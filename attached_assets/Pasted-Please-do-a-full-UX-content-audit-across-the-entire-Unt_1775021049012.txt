Please do a full UX/content audit across the entire Untangle app for the same class of problems we have been noticing.

Main request:
Review all flows — especially Before, After, quick untangle, follow-up questions, untangle moments, stop lines, and CTA buttons — and check for structural, tonal, and UX inconsistencies.

We are seeing repeated issues such as:
- the app narrows too early
- first-tap choices become overcommitted interpretations
- reflections sound too definitive
- some flows feel like questionnaires or symptom checkers
- some branches over-explain without actually helping the user land
- some CTAs promise closure before the user feels settled
- some option sets feel tonally inconsistent with the rest of the screen
- some copy is too long, too analytical, too clinical, or too abstract for the emotional state the user is in

Please audit the whole app for these issues, not just one branch.

What to review across all flows:
1. Option length and visual rhythm
   - check whether any options are noticeably too long, too dense, or too visually disruptive compared to the rest
   - shorten or split where needed
   - keep option sets feeling parallel and coherent

2. First-tap logic
   - check whether first-layer options are being treated as signals or as conclusions
   - revise branches that lock into one interpretation too quickly

3. Follow-up question quality
   - check whether second-step questions genuinely open exploration or prematurely narrow the user into a single frame
   - avoid making follow-ups feel like diagnostic funnels

4. Untangle moment copy
   - check whether reflections sound like grounded, gentle hypotheses rather than verdicts
   - soften anything that feels overly conclusive, therapist-like, or “I know what you really mean”

5. Emotional landing
   - check whether flows actually help the user feel more settled, or only provide interpretation
   - revise places where the app names the knot but does not help the user come down from it

6. CTA honesty
   - review buttons like “Close this loop” and similar language
   - make sure CTA wording matches what the user actually feels at that point
   - avoid promising closure too early

7. Tone consistency
   - check whether the app stays emotionally precise, grounded, low-shame, and usable
   - remove anything that sounds like a diet app, symptom checker, generic self-help coach, budgeting app, or overly abstract therapist bot

8. UI-pattern consistency
   - check whether screens feel too much like forms or quizzes
   - identify where the experience should feel more like untangling and less like answering labeled boxes

Specific issue to fix right now:
One of the “after eating” options is too long and visually jarring:
“I ate something not worth it, and now I’m scared I won’t have room for the meal I actually care about”

This option is emotionally real, but too long and too visually disruptive compared to the other choices.
Please rewrite it into a shorter, cleaner option that preserves the meaning.

Good directions could be things like:
- I ate something not worth it, and now I regret the space it took
- I ate something not worth it, and now I feel like I wasted room
- I’m stuck on the fact that it took room from what I actually wanted
- I ate something I didn’t really want, and now I regret using the room
- I feel like I used up room on the wrong thing

Please review all other option sets for similar outliers.

- Each branch = lens, not verdict
- Insight should not become overclassification
- Naming should lead toward softening and landing
- Keep copy concise, emotionally true, and psychologically accurate
- Preserve Untangle’s original direction as an emotional-support / rumination-interruption product
