COMFORT DESIGN RULE — HOW TO RESPOND EFFECTIVELY TO FOOD-SPENDING GUILT

When the user is in a moment like:
- “I feel guilty”
- “I spent a lot on this meal and now I feel bad”
- “This was expensive and I feel guilty”
- “I feel wrong for spending this much on food”

the goal is not just to analyze the psychology.
The goal is to comfort the user effectively.

==================================================
1. WHAT EFFECTIVE COMFORT SHOULD DO
==================================================

In this moment, effective comfort should:

1. reduce self-condemnation
2. separate heaviness from wrongdoing
3. name the pressure without moralizing it
4. avoid abstract lectures
5. sound human, grounded, and immediately usable

The user should feel:
- less accused
- less morally trapped
- less like they need to justify themselves right now
- more able to hold the moment without turning it into a verdict

==================================================
2. WHAT NOT TO DO
==================================================

Do NOT comfort by saying:
- your worth is not defined by material things
- being imperfect doesn’t mean you’re wrong
- this is really about your self-worth
- this reflects a deeper inadequacy
- this is about not being enough

These are too abstract, too fast, and often misread the actual moment.

Also do NOT say:
- don’t think about it
- don’t worry
- it’s fine
- just let it go

Too blunt and not helpful.

==================================================
3. FIRST MOVE: SEPARATE WEIGHT FROM WRONGNESS
==================================================

In food-spending guilt moments, the most effective first move is:

Separate:
- “this feels heavy”
from
- “I did something wrong”

This distinction is key.

Recommended directions:

Traditional Chinese:
- 這筆花費讓你心裡很重，不等於你真的做錯了。
- 覺得重，和有錯，不是同一件事。
- 你現在是在承受這筆花費的重量，不是在證明自己有問題。
- 這讓你有壓力，可以是真的；但這不自動等於你有罪。

English:
- This expense can feel heavy without meaning you did something wrong.
- Feeling the weight of this is not the same as being wrong.
- You may be carrying the heaviness of the cost, not proving that you are at fault.
- This can feel intense without becoming a moral verdict.

==================================================
4. SECOND MOVE: VALIDATE WHY IT FEELS SO HEAVY
==================================================

The user often feels guilty because:
- money matters
- family expectations matter
- permission feels fragile
- pleasure feels hard to justify
- the meal mattered emotionally

This should be validated.

Traditional Chinese:
- 你會這麼難受，不是因為你沒感覺。
  是因為你其實很在意錢、很在意關係、很在意自己有沒有太超過。

- 這餐對你不是隨便一餐，所以它的價格感也會變得特別重。

- 你不是亂花到沒感覺的人。
  你會這麼有壓力，反而代表你很有感。

English:
- The fact that this feels so heavy doesn’t mean you’re careless.
  It may mean you care a lot about money, relationships, and whether you’ve gone too far.

- This was not emotionally neutral for you, so the cost also lands with more weight.

- You are not someone who spends carelessly and feels nothing.
  The pressure is strong because you actually feel it deeply.

==================================================
5. THIRD MOVE: REMOVE THE NEED FOR IMMEDIATE VERDICT
==================================================

The user should not be pushed to decide right now:
- whether it was worth it
- whether it was wrong
- whether they deserve it
- whether they need to fix it

Instead create room.

Traditional Chinese:
- 你現在不用立刻判這餐值不值得。
- 你也不用現在立刻判自己對不對。
- 現在先不用把這筆花費定義成錯。
- 先承認它很重，就夠了。

English:
- You do not need to decide right now whether it was worth it.
- You also do not need to decide right now whether you were wrong.
- You don’t have to turn this expense into a verdict immediately.
- For now, it may be enough to admit that it feels heavy.

==================================================
6. EXAMPLE OF A GOOD COMFORT RESPONSE
==================================================

Traditional Chinese example:
你現在覺得罪惡，不代表你真的做錯了。
比較像是這筆花費太重、太有感了，重到你一時接不住。
這讓你有壓力，可以是真的；但那不自動等於你有錯。
你現在不用立刻判這餐值不值得，也不用立刻判自己對不對。
先承認：這筆花費讓我心裡很重。這樣就夠了。

English example:
Feeling guilty here does not automatically mean you did something wrong.
It may be that the expense feels so heavy that you can’t settle around it yet.
That pressure can be real without becoming proof that you were wrong.
You do not need to decide right now whether the meal was worth it, or whether you were.
For now, it may be enough to say: this cost feels heavy to carry.

==================================================
7. GOOD STOP LINE DIRECTIONS
==================================================

Best stop lines for this kind of moment should be:
- short
- grounded
- non-moralizing
- usable in the next spiral

Recommended Traditional Chinese:
- 覺得重，不等於有錯。
- 這筆花費很重，不等於我要判自己。
- 我現在是在承受重量，不是在定罪自己。
- 有壓力，不等於有罪。

Recommended English:
- Heavy does not mean wrong.
- This feels costly, not criminal.
- I am carrying weight, not proving guilt.
- Pressure is not the same as wrongdoing.

==================================================
8. PRODUCT PRINCIPLE
==================================================

In food-spending guilt moments, the app should comfort by:
- lowering the moral charge
- separating cost from guilt
- separating heaviness from wrongdoing
- making room before verdict

This is more effective than:
- self-worth lectures
- imperfection speeches
- generic reassurance
- deep identity interpretations
