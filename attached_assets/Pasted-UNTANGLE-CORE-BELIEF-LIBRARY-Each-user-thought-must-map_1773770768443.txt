UNTANGLE CORE BELIEF LIBRARY

Each user thought must map to ONE of the following loops.
Do NOT invent new categories.

---

1. WRONG CHOICE LOOP
User fears making the wrong decision.

Belief:
"If I choose wrong, it means something is wrong with me."

Examples:
- trying to pick the right option
- comparing menus
- can't decide what to eat

Insight:
"You’re not choosing a meal.
You’re trying not to choose wrong."

---

2. REGRET LOOP
User fears future regret.

Belief:
"If I regret this, it means I failed."

Examples:
- worried I'll regret it
- what if there's something better

Insight:
"You’re not choosing food.
You’re trying to avoid regret."

---

3. WORTHINESS LOOP ⭐（你這題就是這個）
User questions if they deserve the experience.

Belief:
"I have to earn the right to enjoy things."

Examples:
- I shouldn't spend money
- I don't deserve this
- it's too expensive for me

Insight:
"You’re not thinking about the food.
You’re questioning whether you deserve it."

---

4. BURDEN LOOP ⭐（你這題也有）
User feels they are a burden to others.

Belief:
"If I cost money, I become a burden."

Examples:
- I feel bad spending my parents' money
- I shouldn't make them pay
- I feel like a burden

Insight:
"You’re not thinking about the meal.
You’re afraid of being a burden."

---

5. CONTROL LOOP
User fears losing control.

Belief:
"If I lose control, everything falls apart."

Examples:
- if I start eating I can't stop
- I need to control intake

Insight:
"You’re not thinking about food.
You’re trying to stay in control."

---

6. SCARCITY LOOP
User fears wasting resources.

Belief:
"Resources are limited, I must not waste them."

Examples:
- food is too expensive
- not worth the price

Insight:
"You’re not thinking about the price.
You’re afraid of wasting resources."

---

7. PERFECTION LOOP
User wants the optimal choice.

Belief:
"There is a perfect choice, and I must find it."

Examples:
- best option
- optimal choice

Insight:
"You’re not choosing a meal.
You’re trying to find the perfect one."

---

8. COMPARISON LOOP
User compares options endlessly.

Belief:
"If I don't compare everything, I'll miss something better."

Examples:
- comparing dishes
- menu scanning

Insight:
"You’re not choosing.
You’re stuck comparing."

---

9. FOMO LOOP
User fears missing out.

Belief:
"If I miss something better, I lose."

Examples:
- what if something else is better

Insight:
"You’re not choosing food.
You’re afraid of missing out."

---

10. VALIDATION LOOP
User wants confirmation they are correct.

Belief:
"I need to know I made the right decision."

Examples:
- was that the right choice
- replaying decision

Insight:
"You’re not thinking about the meal.
You’re checking if you were right."

---

11. SAFETY LOOP ⭐（你這題有）
User feels unsafe or unstable.

Belief:
"If I'm not safe, I must restrict or control."

Examples:
- I feel unsafe alone
- anxiety around eating alone

Insight:
"You’re not thinking about the food.
You’re trying to feel safe."

---

12. SELF-WORTH LOOP
User ties behavior to identity.

Belief:
"My choices define my value."

Examples:
- I'm bad for eating this
- I shouldn't have done that

Insight:
"You’re not thinking about the action.
You’re judging yourself."
