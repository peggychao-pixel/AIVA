IMPORTANT PRODUCT UPDATE — INTEGRATE REAL DINNER UNCERTAINTY, SCARCITY, AND HUNGER INTO THE APP

Please update the product logic based on this refined problem understanding:

The product should not assume that all food-related distress is mainly about craving, perfectionism, post-meal rumination, or emotional closure alone.

A more accurate model is:

For some users with a history of bingeing, loss-of-control eating, or food-related distress, dinner is not just a meal.
It functions as a physical and psychological anchor.

When dinner feels uncertain — especially under conditions like:
- real physical hunger
- warm food not being guaranteed
- limited food options
- stores closing soon
- being unable to go back out
- unfamiliar environment
- money being tight
- food access feeling unstable

the user may enter a state of:
- urgency
- scarcity thinking
- anticipatory distress
- self-numbing or coping behavior
- intense mental preoccupation

In these moments, the user may not mainly be seeking pleasure.
She may be seeking:
- safety
- warmth
- fullness
- relief
- something reliable to hold onto

This is very important.

==================================================
1. PRODUCT POSITIONING UPDATE
==================================================

Do NOT frame the app only as:
- a rumination interruption tool
- a guilt support tool
- a closure / ending tool
- a craving management tool

Instead expand the product’s understanding to include:
- dinner uncertainty
- resource scarcity
- real hunger
- unstable food access
- warm nourishment as an anchor
- urgency caused by conditions, not just psychology

This should deepen the product, not replace its identity.

The app is still a lightweight, moment-based support tool,
but it must recognize that some eating distress is intensified by real-world conditions, not just internal interpretation.

==================================================
2. NEW INTERPRETATION RULE
==================================================

When the user is distressed around food, do not assume the problem is always:
- wanting the best thing
- needing closure
- overthinking
- self-worth panic

Sometimes the deeper issue is:
- I am really hungry
- I do not know if I will get enough food
- I do not know if I will get warm food
- my options are shrinking
- I do not feel secure that I will be fed
- money makes comfort or nourishment feel risky

The product must be able to recognize:
This may be a safety / nourishment / access problem,
not only a meaning-making problem.

==================================================
3. SPECIFIC USER STATES TO ADD
==================================================

Please add or revise product states that cover:

A. DINNER UNCERTAINTY
Examples:
- I don’t know what dinner will be
- I’m scared there won’t be enough
- I don’t know if I’ll get something warm
- I can feel options shrinking

B. HUNGER + URGENCY
Examples:
- I’m getting too hungry and everything feels worse
- I can feel my body panicking
- I need something reliable soon
- I’m not just emotional — I’m underfed and overwhelmed

C. SCARCITY + ACCESS STRESS
Examples:
- stores are closing
- I can’t go back out later
- money is tight
- I don’t know if I can get what I need
- I’m in an unfamiliar place and I don’t feel secure about food

These should not be flattened into generic anxiety or generic craving.

==================================================
4. PRODUCT DESIGN IMPLICATION
==================================================

The app should be able to distinguish between moments where the user needs:

A. Emotional untangling
vs.
B. Immediate stabilization around food certainty, access, warmth, or enoughness

That means the product may need branches that do things like:
- reduce panic caused by shrinking options
- acknowledge real hunger
- help the user identify what is truly needed right now
- reduce moralization when the body is under stress
- support the user in moving toward a reliable next step, not just a reflective insight

==================================================
5. WHAT THE PRODUCT SHOULD SAY IN THESE MOMENTS
==================================================

Good directions:

Traditional Chinese:
- 你現在不一定只是在想太多，可能也是真的太餓、太不確定了。
- 當晚餐沒有把握、選項在縮、身體又很餓時，整個系統本來就會更警戒。
- 你現在想找的，不一定是享受，可能是可靠、溫熱、夠用、能讓身體先放心下來的東西。
- 這不只是情緒問題，這裡面也有真實的飢餓、現實限制、和食物不確定性。

English:
- This may not just be overthinking. You may also be genuinely too hungry and too uncertain.
- When dinner is unclear, options are shrinking, and your body is already very hungry, it makes sense that everything feels more urgent.
- You may not mainly be seeking pleasure right now. You may be seeking something reliable, warm, and sufficient enough for your body to stop panicking.
- This is not only emotional. Real hunger and real uncertainty may be part of the distress.

==================================================
6. DO NOT OVER-PSYCHOLOGIZE
==================================================

In these states, do NOT jump too quickly to:
- self-worth
- perfectionism
- identity-level shame
- abstract closure theory
- “you’re really afraid of being not enough”

Sometimes the user’s distress is being amplified by:
- real hunger
- lack of access
- unreliable dinner plans
- money constraints
- lack of warm food
- unstable environment

The product must earn the right to go deeper.
It should first recognize the concrete conditions.

- “I’m really hungry and I don’t know what dinner is”
- “I’m afraid there won’t be enough”

==================================================

Do not turn the app into:
- a meal planning app
- a calorie tracker
- a budgeting app
- a generic food finder

Keep it as a lightweight moment-based support tool.

But allow it to say:
“This moment may need certainty and stabilization, not just interpretation.”
