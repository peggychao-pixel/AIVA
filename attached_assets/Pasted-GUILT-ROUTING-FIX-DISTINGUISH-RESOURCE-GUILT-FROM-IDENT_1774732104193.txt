GUILT ROUTING FIX — DISTINGUISH RESOURCE GUILT FROM IDENTITY GUILT

There is a major routing problem in the current guilt flow.

The system currently over-interprets guilt as:
- “something is wrong with me”
- “I crossed a moral line”
- “I am imperfect / wrong”
- identity-level shame

But many guilt moments are not identity guilt.
Some are specifically about money, spending, permission, or relational burden.

Example:
If the user says they feel guilty after spending a lot of money on food,
the system should NOT immediately jump to:
- “does this make me wrong?”
- “something’s wrong with you”
- “being imperfect doesn’t mean I’m wrong”

That may be inaccurate.

==================================================
1. NEW RULE: SPLIT GUILT INTO SUBTYPES
==================================================

Do not treat all guilt as the same.

At minimum, distinguish between:

A. Identity guilt / moralized self-blame
- I feel like I did something wrong
- I feel like I crossed a line
- I feel like this says something bad about me

B. Resource guilt / spending guilt
- I spent too much
- I feel bad about the money
- This felt too expensive
- I feel guilty for using resources this way

C. Relational guilt
- I feel bad because of my parents / family / what others gave up
- I feel guilty receiving / spending / asking
- I feel like I’m burdening someone

D. Appetite / eating guilt
- I ate too much
- I overdid it
- I feel guilty after eating

These should not all route to the same identity-shame logic.

==================================================
2. SPECIFIC RULE FOR FOOD-SPENDING GUILT
==================================================

If the guilt is about:
- spending a lot on food
- expensive meals
- using money on self-pleasure
- food as luxury
- tension with family money rules

then first interpret it as RESOURCE GUILT or RELATIONAL GUILT,
not identity shame.

Possible insight directions:

Traditional Chinese:
- 你現在的罪惡感，不一定是在說你做錯了。
  可能是在說這筆花費對你來說太重了。

- 你不一定是在怪自己這個人。
  你比較像是在承受這筆支出帶來的壓力和不安。

- 這個 guilt 可能不是「我有問題」，
  而是「我不知道自己能不能承受這樣花」。

- 你現在卡的，可能不是這餐本身，
  而是這筆錢花下去之後，你心裡沒有空間好好接住它。

English:
- This guilt may not be telling you that you’re a bad person.
  It may be telling you that the cost feels heavy to carry.

- You may not be judging yourself as a person.
  You may be reacting to the weight of the spending.

- This may be less about “something is wrong with me”
  and more about “I don’t know how to feel okay about this expense.”

- The hardest part may not be the meal itself,
  but not knowing how to emotionally hold what it cost.

==================================================
3. DO NOT JUMP TO “YOU ARE WRONG / NOT ENOUGH”
==================================================

For money-related guilt, do NOT automatically generate:
- whether this makes you wrong
- whether something is wrong with you
- being imperfect doesn’t mean I’m wrong
- your worth is not defined by this

These may fit some moments, but they are too identity-heavy for straightforward spending guilt.

==================================================
4. BETTER STOP LINE DIRECTIONS FOR RESOURCE GUILT
==================================================

For food-spending guilt, better stop lines include:

Traditional Chinese:
- 這筆花費讓我不安，不等於我做錯了。
- 我現在在承受花費的重量，不用立刻把它判成錯。
- 覺得貴、覺得重，和我有沒有錯，不是同一件事。
- 我可以先承認這筆錢讓我心裡很重，不用立刻判自己。

English:
- This expense can feel heavy without meaning I did something wrong.
- Feeling the weight of the cost is not the same as being wrong.
- This can feel expensive and unsettling without becoming a moral verdict.
- I can admit this cost feels heavy without turning it into self-condemnation.

==================================================
5. IF FAMILY / PARENTS ARE IMPLIED, CONSIDER RELATIONAL GUILT
==================================================

If spending guilt includes:
- dad / mom / parents
- family rules
- fear of disappointing family
- guilt about using their money

then consider RELATIONAL GUILT before identity guilt.

==================================================
6. PRODUCT PRINCIPLE
==================================================

Do not over-psychologize practical guilt.

Sometimes guilt is not a hidden self-worth wound.
Sometimes it is a concrete reaction to money, permission, scarcity, or relational pressure.

The system must earn the right to go deeper.
It should not skip there automatically.
