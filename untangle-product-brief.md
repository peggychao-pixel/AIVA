# Untangle — Product Strategy Brief

---

## 1. Product Problem

People with eating-related rumination are not struggling with a lack of information, willpower, or better rules. They are struggling with the absence of a soft, non-rigid structure that helps them reach completion — without triggering self-surveillance, rigidity, or rebound.

The vacuum around the food moment is the problem. Not the food itself.

Specifically:
- The suffering happens *during* eating, not only after — in the anticipation of losing control, not in the loss itself
- Home environments strip away the natural stopping cues (the bill, the last course, the leaving moment) that restaurants provide
- More monitoring, more logging, and more rules make the loop worse for this user, not better — for some people, discipline is part of the loop

---

## 2. Core User Insight

The real desire is not to eat less, eat healthier, or follow a better plan.

It is to arrive at **"enough"** — with less internal friction.

"Enough" is reached not only through quantity, but through:
- Timing that feels complete
- A rhythm that felt held
- An ending that felt clean
- Not having to keep deciding
- Not having to keep judging

The user is not chasing less food. They are chasing completion.

---

## 3. Why Existing Solutions Miss

| What they offer | Why it fails this user |
|---|---|
| Calorie tracking, logging | Increases surveillance; becomes another loop to manage |
| Meal plans, rules, portions | Rigidity creates rebound; discipline is already the problem |
| General mindfulness / breathing | Too abstract; doesn't address the specific moment of fear |
| Therapy / clinical support | Useful but not available in the moment; not designed for micro-interruption |
| Journaling apps | Reflective, not interruptive; arrives too late |
| Habit apps | Assumes motivation and compliance; this user has neither in that moment |

The core miss: most products assume the user lacks information or self-control. This user has too much of both. What they lack is a container that can hold the moment without demanding more judgment from them.

---

## 4. Product Opportunity

A moment-based rumination interruption tool that:

- Meets the user at the exact high-risk moment (before / during / after a food decision)
- Asks one precise question to name the loop — not to analyze it, not to solve it
- Provides a stop line: a single grounding sentence that interrupts the spiral without requiring willpower
- Creates a soft ending for the moment — completion without perfection
- Reduces self-surveillance rather than adding to it

No tracking. No scoring. No rules. Just a structure that helps the moment close.

---

## 5. Design Principles

**Container, not correction.** The product holds the moment with structure, not judgment. It does not try to fix the behavior.

**Completion over control.** The goal is to help the user reach an ending, not to enforce a correct choice.

**Interrupt, don't analyze.** Insight is useful only if it creates relief. If it increases pressure, it is the wrong kind of insight.

**Reduce surveillance.** Every feature must ask: does this add to self-monitoring? If yes, remove it.

**Non-rigid structure.** Soft enough to not trigger rigidity. Present enough to reduce the vacuum.

**Earned, not imposed.** The user should arrive at the realization themselves. Nothing is lectured. Nothing is prescribed.

---

## 6. What This Product Is NOT

- Not a therapy app — it does not provide clinical support or treatment
- Not a calorie tracker or food logger — it does not measure, score, or quantify intake
- Not a meal planner — it does not tell the user what to eat
- Not a habit or discipline app — it does not reward compliance or streak behavior
- Not a generic mental wellness app — it is tightly scoped to eating-related rumination moments
- Not a journaling app — it does not ask the user to reflect; it asks them to interrupt

---

## 7. One-Sentence Positioning

**Untangle is a rumination interruption tool for eating-related loops — helping users reach "enough" with less self-surveillance and less rebound, one moment at a time.**

---

## 8. User Journey Map

```
[High-risk moment arrives]
  ↓
Fear of losing control activates (before any action)
  ↓
Self-monitoring turns on → rigidity rises
  ↓
↙                          ↘
[Without Untangle]        [With Untangle]
More rules, more guilt     One question names the loop
Rebound urge rises         Stop line interrupts the spiral
Post-eating rumination     Moment reaches a soft close
Future fear increases      Less self-attack afterward
Loop runs longer next time Loop runs shorter next time
```

The product's job is not to prevent the moment. It is to help the moment close — cleanly, without requiring the user to be their own monitor, judge, and decision-maker all at once.
