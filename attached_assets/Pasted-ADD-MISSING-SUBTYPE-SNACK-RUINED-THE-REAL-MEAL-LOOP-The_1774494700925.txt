ADD MISSING SUBTYPE — SNACK RUINED THE REAL MEAL LOOP

There is a missing subtype in the current selection screen.

Current options cover:
- choosing the same thing again
- eating but not feeling satisfied
- partially fixing it but it still not counting
- generic stuckness

But they do NOT cover this very common loop:

The user ate a small snack or mediocre food item before the real meal,
and is now stuck because:
- it did not feel worth it
- it may reduce appetite
- it may ruin space for the meal that actually mattered
- the wrong thing got there first

This is a distinct subtype.

==================================================
NEW SUBTYPE
==================================================

Name:
SNACK STOLE THE MOMENT LOOP
or
PREMEAL INTERFERENCE LOOP

Definition:
The user is not mainly stuck on what they ate.
They are stuck because something not-worth-it may have taken space away from the meal that actually mattered more.

Core mechanism:
unworthy thing entered first + fear of lost appetite/space + fear of ruining the “real” meal

==================================================
NEW OPTION TO ADD
==================================================

English:
I ate something not worth it, and now I’m worried it ruined the meal I actually wanted

Alternative shorter version:
I ate a mediocre snack, and now I’m scared I won’t have room for the meal I actually care about

Traditional Chinese:
我剛剛吃了一個不太值得的東西，現在怕它把後面真正想吃的那餐弄壞

Alternative:
我先吃了一點，但現在怕沒胃留給真正想吃的餐

==================================================
INSIGHT DIRECTION FOR THIS SUBTYPE
==================================================

Traditional Chinese:
- 你現在卡的不是那塊 snack 本身。
  是它不夠值得，卻可能先把後面那餐的空間佔掉。

- 你不是只在想剛剛吃了什麼。
  你是在怕：不夠好的東西先進來了，真正重要的那餐會不會被它弄壞。

English:
- You may not be stuck on the snack itself.
  You may be stuck on the fear that something not worth it got in first and took space away from the meal that actually mattered.

- This may not be about having eaten a little.
  It may be about fearing that the wrong thing got there first and spoiled the version you were actually waiting for.

==================================================
UI RULE
==================================================

Do not force this scenario into generic “I’m still stuck.”
It deserves its own subtype and routing path.

END
