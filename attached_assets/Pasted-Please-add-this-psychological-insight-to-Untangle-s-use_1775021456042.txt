Please add this psychological insight to Untangle’s user model:

The user may genuinely crave strong, rich, high-intensity foods — not because she is reckless, but because bland or low-intensity foods may feel too weak to hold her.

Sometimes she wants something salty, heavy, rich, warm, or high-impact because she needs immediate sensory and emotional landing.
She may even want to eat quickly, standing up, or wherever she is, because the urgency is not just hunger — it is a need to be caught fast.

But at the same time, these foods may also trigger internal conflict:
- “I want this”
- “I shouldn’t eat this”
- “This is too much”
- “I need to limit this”
- “I’m already in a struggle while eating”

This creates a painful split:
the foods that feel most likely to satisfy or hold her may also be the foods most likely to trigger shame, self-surveillance, or restriction.

Another important layer:
She may feel ashamed eating in front of other people not simply because of the food itself, but because she does not want others to witness the inner struggle attached to it.

The shame may come from:
- feeling visibly pulled toward the food
- not feeling natural or relaxed while eating
- feeling greedy, messy, intense, or out of control
- feeling exposed in her conflicted relationship with food

Please make sure Untangle understands:
This is not just “craving bad food.”
It is often a conflict between:
- needing strong sensory/emotional holding
and
- fearing what that need means, looks like, or triggers

Helpful language direction:
- “Sometimes what you want most is also what feels hardest to eat without shame.”
- “The intensity of the craving may come from needing stronger holding, not from moral failure.”
- “Part of what feels exposing may be the struggle around the food, not just the food itself.”
- “It may feel hard to eat this in front of people because it does not feel neutral to you.”

Please reflect this in:
- craving-related flows
- shame-related flows
- public-eating / being seen / embarrassment branches
- interpretation logic around high-intensity foods
