IMPORTANT PRODUCT INSIGHT UPDATE — PRE-MEAL FAILURE-PREVENTION MODE

Please update the product logic, framing, and feature design based on this more accurate insight:

The user’s distress before eating is not just ordinary anticipation and not just general anxiety.
It is often a failure-prevention state.

The user already knows how painful the aftermath can be if the meal or evening does not “land” well.
Because of that, before eating, she is often not simply deciding what she wants.
She is trying to prevent:
- a failed ending
- painful rumination later
- unfinishedness at night
- being mentally “hung” for hours
- the need to keep repairing the evening
- difficulty sleeping because the day never felt complete

This means the pre-meal phase is often:
not craving
not simple indecision
but high-alert prevention of a known painful spiral.

==================================================
1. UPDATE THE CORE PROBLEM FRAMING
==================================================

Do not frame the product only as:
- post-meal rumination support
- craving interruption
- eating guilt support
- anticipatory anxiety before meals

A more accurate framing is:

The product supports users who enter a high-alert failure-prevention mode before eating, because they fear that a bad or incomplete ending will lead to intense rumination and distress later.

Preferred wording:
The user is not only trying to choose dinner.
She is trying to prevent a known pattern in which a failed ending leads to severe emotional aftermath.

Plain-language version:
The pain is not just “what should I eat?”
It is “how do I stop tonight from turning into a painful unfinished mess later?”

==================================================
2. PRODUCT TIMING IMPLICATION
==================================================

This means the most important intervention moment may be:
BEFORE the meal,
when the user starts bracing against failure.

The product should not only activate after the user is already spiraling.
It should also support the earlier moment when the user starts:
- scanning
- comparing
- searching
- planning
- pre-repairing
- mentally rehearsing outcomes
- worrying about how to make the night land safely

==================================================

==================================================

Add a new subtype or entry path:

Definition:
The user is not only anxious about the meal.

- I can already feel myself trying to avoid a bad ending

- 我不是在想吃什麼，我是在防止這晚變得很難收尾

==================================================
4. IMPORTANT DISTINCTION
==================================================

Do not flatten this into:
- generic pre-meal anxiety
- simple fear of choosing wrong
- craving for the “right” food
- perfectionism alone

This state is more specific:
The user is actively trying to prevent a known, painful emotional aftermath.

That is why the pre-meal distress can be so intense.
The panic is not random.
It is trying to protect against a future crash.

==================================================
5. FEATURE IMPLICATIONS
==================================================

Design features for the pre-meal phase that help the user:
- reduce failure-prevention vigilance
- feel less like the whole night depends on one perfect choice
- reduce the need to pre-repair the entire evening
- move from alarm into something more held and navigable
- feel like the night does not need to be perfectly secured in advance

Do NOT solve this with:
- meal optimization
- more comparisons
- more food analysis
- rules
- calorie logic
- “just choose something”
- generic grounding

Focus instead on:
- soft containment before the meal
- reducing the terror of a bad ending
- helping the user feel that one imperfect meal does not automatically doom the whole night
- loosening the feeling that the entire evening has to be engineered in advance

==================================================

Good stop lines for this subtype should reduce the feeling that the whole night must be perfectly prevented in advance.

Traditional Chinese:

- 我現在不用先把整晚救完，才能往下走。

English:
- I don’t need to secure the whole night before it begins.
- I don’t need to save the entire evening in advance.
- This meal matters, but it does not have to determine the whole night.
- I am trying to prevent pain right now, not actually seeing the future.

Problem statement:
The problem is not only post-meal rumination. It often begins earlier, when the user enters a failure-prevention mode before eating, trying to avoid a painful emotional aftermath later in the night.

Why it matters:
This matters because the user may spend the entire day in vigilance, search, and mental planning, not because the meal has already gone badly, but because she fears how painful it will be if the night does not end well.

Key uncertainty to test:
Whether the deepest pain is best described as pre-meal failure-prevention against a known rumination spiral, rather than general anxiety, craving, or perfectionism.
