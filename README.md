# Untangle

Untangle is a behavior-sensitive rumination interruption tool for eating-related loops.

It is designed for moments when the problem is not food itself, but the fear, self-judgment, replay, and decision paralysis surrounding it. Instead of adding more tracking, rules, or self-surveillance, Untangle helps the user reach **"enough"** with less internal friction.

## The Problem

People with eating-related rumination are not primarily struggling with a lack of information, willpower, or better rules.

They are struggling with the absence of a soft, non-rigid structure that helps them reach completion without triggering more self-surveillance, rigidity, or rebound.

The problem is often not the food itself.  
The problem is the vacuum around the food moment.

More specifically:

- The distress often begins **before** eating, in the anticipation of losing control
- Home environments remove the natural stopping cues that restaurants often provide
- More monitoring, more logging, and more rules can make the loop worse, not better

---

## Core User Insight

The real desire is not simply to eat less, eat healthier, or follow a better plan.

It is to arrive at **"enough"** with less internal friction.

For this user, enough is not reached only through quantity. It is also reached through:

- timing that feels complete
- a rhythm that felt held
- an ending that felt clean
- not having to keep deciding
- not having to keep judging

The user is not chasing less food.  
The user is chasing **completion**.

---

## Why Existing Solutions Miss

| What they offer | Why it fails this user |
|---|---|
| Calorie tracking / logging | Increases surveillance and becomes another loop to manage |
| Meal plans / portions / rules | Rigidity creates rebound; discipline is often already part of the loop |
| General mindfulness | Too abstract for the actual moment of fear |
| Therapy / clinical support | Valuable, but not available in the micro-moment |
| Journaling apps | Reflective rather than interruptive |
| Habit apps | Assume motivation and compliance that may not exist in that moment |

Most products assume the user lacks information or self-control.  
This user often has too much of both. What they lack is a container that can hold the moment without demanding more judgment from them.

---

## Product Opportunity

Untangle is a moment-based interruption tool that:

- meets the user at the exact high-risk moment
- names the loop with one precise question
- offers a psychologically accurate insight
- provides a short stop line the user can actually carry with them
- helps the moment reach a soft close
- reduces self-surveillance rather than adding to it

No tracking. No scoring. No rules.  
Just a structure that helps the moment close.

---

## Design Principles

- **Container, not correction.**  
  The product holds the moment with structure, not judgment.

- **Completion over control.**  
  The goal is not the correct choice. The goal is a clean enough ending.

- **Interrupt, don't overanalyze.**  
  Insight matters only if it reduces pressure.

- **Reduce surveillance.**  
  If a feature increases self-monitoring, it likely does not belong.

- **Non-rigid structure.**  
  Soft enough not to trigger rigidity, but present enough to reduce the vacuum.

- **Earned, not imposed.**  
  The user should arrive at the realization, not be lectured into it.

---

## What This Product Is Not

Untangle is **not**:

- a therapy app
- a calorie tracker
- a food log
- a meal planner
- a streak / discipline app
- a generic mental wellness app
- a journaling app

It is a tightly scoped support tool for eating-related rumination moments.

## Current Product Experience

The current prototype includes:

- a homepage that lets users choose where the loop is happening
  - Before eating
  - After eating
  - It's bigger than the food
  - My mind won't let it go
- structured guided flows for naming the loop
- short psychologically precise "Untangle moments"
- stop lines / anchor lines that aim to reduce loop pressure
- moment history and saved moment views
- bilingual interface support (Traditional Chinese / English)
