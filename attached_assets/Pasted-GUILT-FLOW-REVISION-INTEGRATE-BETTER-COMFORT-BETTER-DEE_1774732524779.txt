GUILT FLOW REVISION — INTEGRATE BETTER COMFORT, BETTER DEEPENING, AND LANGUAGE CONSISTENCY

Please revise the guilt flow using all of the following rules together.

==================================================
A. CORE PROBLEM
==================================================

The current guilt flow still has three weaknesses:

1. It overroutes guilt into “wrong person / flaw / imperfection / worth” too quickly.
2. The second layer often repeats the first layer instead of adding new mechanism.
3. Language consistency is still broken across the screen.

The goal is to make guilt responses:
- more emotionally accurate
- less repetitive
- less identity-heavy by default
- more comforting in a grounded way
- more product-usable

==================================================
B. DO NOT TREAT ALL GUILT AS IDENTITY GUILT
==================================================

Do not automatically interpret guilt as:
- I am wrong
- something is wrong with me
- I am flawed
- I am not enough
- my worth is damaged

Sometimes guilt is:
- resource guilt
- spending guilt
- relational guilt
- eating guilt
- fear of having crossed a line
- fear of loss of control
- fear of what this moment opens up

You must earn the right to escalate to identity-level shame.
Do not jump there automatically.

==================================================
C. EFFECTIVE COMFORT RULE
==================================================

When the user says “I feel guilty,” the first-layer response should comfort effectively by:

1. separating heaviness from wrongdoing
2. lowering self-condemnation
3. avoiding lectures
4. avoiding self-worth slogans
5. not treating discomfort as proof of guilt

Preferred direction:
- feeling this as heavy does not automatically mean the user did something wrong
- the user may be carrying weight, not proving fault
- the user does not need an immediate verdict

Good first-layer directions:
- This may feel heavy without meaning you did something wrong.
- Feeling this much weight does not automatically make it a verdict.
- You may be carrying a strong sense of wrongness, but that does not make it true.

Traditional Chinese equivalents:
- 這很重，不等於你真的做錯了。
- 覺得很重，不等於這就是證據。
- 你現在在承受一種很強的錯感，但那不自動代表它是真的。

==================================================
D. FIRST LAYER SHOULD NAME THE MOMENT CLEARLY
==================================================

A good first layer for guilt can include:
- sense of having crossed a line
- moralized discomfort
- the mind translating bad feeling into wrongdoing
- intense heaviness

But it should not over-explain.

Example shape:
You may not just be feeling guilty — you may be feeling like you crossed a line somewhere.
Feeling this weight doesn’t automatically mean you did something wrong.

==================================================
E. DEEPER LAYER MUST ADD NEW MECHANISM
==================================================

The deeper layer must NOT simply restate:
- wrongness
- perfection
- self-judgment
- flaw
- not enough

with new wording.

A valid deeper layer must introduce genuinely new information such as:

1. what guilt is trying to protect
2. what consequence the user fears this moment opens up
3. why the user cannot let go of the guilt
4. what function guilt is serving in the loop

Good deeper directions:
- You may be holding onto guilt because it proves to you that you still care.
- If you stopped feeling bad, it might feel like you had really gone too far.
- The fear may not be just this one moment, but what it seems to open the door to.
- What makes this sticky may be that your mind turns discomfort into proof, and proof into self-judgment.

Traditional Chinese equivalents:
- 你可能捨不得放掉這個 guilt，因為只要還在自責，就像還證明自己有在乎。
- 如果你現在不再覺得糟，心裡反而可能會覺得：那我是不是就真的太超過了。
- 你怕的可能不只是這一刻，而是這一刻好像打開了什麼口。
- 最黏人的地方可能是：你一不舒服，腦子就把它翻成證據，再翻成對自己的判決。

==================================================
F. STOP LINE RULE
==================================================

The stop line should:
- reduce self-condemnation
- stay close to the mechanism
- not become a giant life lesson
- not moralize
- not jump to abstract worth language unless the user already went there

Good stop-line directions:
- Feeling bad is not the same as being wrong.
- This feeling is heavy, but it is not a verdict.
- I don’t need to turn this discomfort into proof.
- 這很重，但不等於定案。
- 不舒服，不等於做錯。
- 這個感覺很重，但它不是判決。

Avoid stop lines like:
- your worth is not defined by this
- being imperfect doesn’t mean you’re wrong
unless the user clearly went to self-worth / imperfection language first.

==================================================
G. DO NOT OVERREPEAT “PERFECTION”
==================================================

Do not use “perfection” in both first and second layer unless absolutely necessary.
It becomes repetitive and generic.

If “perfection” is already implied in the first layer,
the deeper layer should move to:
- protection function
- feared consequence
- why guilt is hard to release

==================================================
H. ONE-SCREEN-ONE-LANGUAGE
==================================================

Fix the language bug fully.

One screen must use one system language only:
- insight card
- stop line
- helper text
- buttons
- deeper prompts
- labels

No English card with Chinese stop line.
No Chinese card with English support copy.

==================================================
I. EXAMPLE OF IMPROVED GUILT FLOW
==================================================

Example first layer:
You may not just be feeling guilty — you may be feeling like you crossed a line somewhere.
But feeling this much weight does not automatically mean you did something wrong.

Example deeper layer:
What may make this so hard to release is that guilt can feel like proof that you still care.
If you stopped feeling bad too soon, it might feel like you had really gone too far.

Example stop line:
This feels heavy, but it is not a verdict.

Traditional Chinese version:
你現在不只是 guilt，可能更像是一種「我是不是越線了」的感覺。
但這種很重的錯感，不自動等於你真的做錯了。

再深一層：
最黏人的地方可能是，你捨不得太快放掉這個 guilt，
因為只要還在自責，就像還證明自己有在乎。
如果太快不難受了，心裡反而可能會怕：那我是不是就真的太超過了。

止住句：
這很重，但不等於定案。

==================================================
J. PRODUCT PRINCIPLE
==================================================

The guilt flow should feel:
- precise
- humane
- less moralizing
- less repetitive
- less like generic psychology content
- more like real emotional guidance in a stuck moment

The user should feel:
- seen
- less condemned
- less trapped in self-verdict
- not talked down to
- not translated too fast into self-worth language
