Please fix the Quick Untangle generation logic. It is drifting far outside Untangle’s intended domain.

Current problem:
The free-text / quick untangle output is sometimes hallucinating generic advice that does not match the user’s actual emotional knot.
For example, it can suddenly reframe the issue as money anxiety, budgeting, or future financial planning, even when that is not what the user meant.
This breaks trust and makes the product feel random and psychologically inaccurate.

Untangle must NOT behave like:
- a budgeting coach
- a generic life advice bot
- a productivity planner
- a self-help app giving broad improvement tips
- a therapist making unrelated interpretations

Untangle SHOULD behave like:
- an emotional clarification tool
- a rumination-interruption tool
- a product that names the knot more clearly
- a product that reflects back the user’s likely inner conflict in grounded, precise language
- a product that helps the user feel seen, not misread

Please add stronger constraints to Quick Untangle generation.

Required rules:
1. Stay close to the user’s actual wording and emotional signal
2. Do not introduce new domains unless the user clearly mentioned them
   - especially do NOT introduce money, budgeting, school planning, productivity, family dynamics, or life management unless explicitly present
3. Do not convert emotional conflict into practical planning advice too early
4. The output should clarify the knot, not solve the user’s life
5. The reflection should feel like:
   - “Here is what may actually be tangled”
   not:
   - “Here is a new topic and action plan”
6. If the model is uncertain, it should stay simple and mirror the emotional pattern rather than inventing a cause
7. Keep the language emotionally precise, gentle, and low-assumption
8. The “Keep this” line should be a psychologically useful anchor sentence, not generic self-help advice

Please revise the generation prompt so Quick Untangle is tightly grounded in Untangle’s actual product purpose.

Helpful product boundary:
Untangle is for naming and loosening inner knots around food, choice, craving, anticipation, guilt, rumination, emotional aftermath, and related self-attack loops.
It is not for broad life coaching.

Also add a fail-safe:
If the generated untangle introduces concepts not present in the user input, regenerate with a stricter “stay close to the user’s language and emotional logic” instruction.
