Please revise the decision-tree logic for Untangle’s “before eating” flow.

Current issue:
Some first-layer options are causing the experience to narrow too early. A user taps one entry point, and the next screen or Untangle moment starts interpreting that choice as the core issue too quickly.

Example:
If the user selects “I’m worried I’ll regret it,” the follow-up flow can quickly overcommit to a single interpretation like replay/regret, when the real experience may still be mixed:
- fear of regret
- fear of choosing wrong
- fear of not feeling satisfied
- fear of waste
- fear of losing control
- fear of not being able to land the ending

This makes the first selection feel too limiting.

Design principle to adopt:
First tap = signal, not conclusion.
Each branch should feel like a lens, not a verdict.

What to change:
- Keep the existing product structure and tone
- Do not redesign Untangle into a different product
- Review the “before eating” branching logic and copy
- Make the first-layer options function as entry points, not diagnoses
- Make second-layer questions open up the experience rather than prematurely narrowing it
- Make Untangle moments sound like gentle hypotheses, not definitive interpretations

Specific guidance:
1. First-layer option = identifies what is active, not what the whole problem is
2. Follow-up questions should expand and clarify, not lock the user into one frame too early
3. Untangle reflections should preserve mixed emotional reality
4. Use softer language such as:
   - “Part of what may be making this hard...”
   - “It sounds like...”
   - “This may be less about X itself, and more about...”
5. Avoid overly deterministic framing like:
   - “You’re not really struggling with X, you’re actually struggling with Y”
6. Make the before-eating flow feel more like the after-eating flow in terms of emotional openness and complexity-holding

Goal:
The user should feel guided, not boxed in.
The experience should help clarify what is happening without collapsing it too soon into a single explanation.
Preserve Untangle’s original emotional-support and untangling direction.
