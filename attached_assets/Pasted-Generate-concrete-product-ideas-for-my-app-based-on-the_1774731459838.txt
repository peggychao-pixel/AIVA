Generate concrete product ideas for my app based on the following user problem.

USER PROBLEM
The user experiences eating distress not primarily because of hunger, but because of unfinished emotional closure around food and nighttime routines.

She is highly sensitive to:
- fragmentation
- lack of containment
- abrupt transitions
- unfinished endings
- the feeling that a meal or evening did not “land”

She often seeks externally structured, aesthetic experiences because they provide:
- pacing
- dignity
- emotional containment
- a graceful ending
- less need to self-monitor

The real need is not more food information.
The real need is help moving from urgency / search / repair mode into:
- completion
- enoughness
- safe landing
- emotional closure

DESIGN DIRECTION
Generate product ideas that help the user:
- feel held without rigid control
- move from “I still need something” into “this can end here”
- reduce the urge to use the next experience to repair the previous one
- create a sense of guided transition at night
- create emotional closure without requiring perfection

IMPORTANT CONSTRAINTS
Do NOT suggest:
- calorie tracking
- meal logging
- macros
- rigid routines
- self-discipline systems
- streaks
- reward/punishment mechanics
- generic self-care advice
- “just journal”
- therapy-style exercises as the main product
- meditation app clichés

Instead focus on:
- closure
- containment
- guided endings
- transition rituals
- soft structure
- pacing
- aesthetic emotional regulation
- reducing fragmentation
- helping the user stop searching for the next thing to make the night feel complete

OUTPUT FORMAT
Generate exactly 12 product ideas.

For each idea, include these sections:

1. Name
A short, memorable feature or concept name.

2. Core moment
What exact moment does this feature help with?
Be specific.

3. User pain it addresses
What specific emotional problem is it solving?
Use precise language, not generic wellness language.

4. How it works
Describe the interaction concretely.
What does the user see, tap, hear, or move through?

5. Why it helps
Explain the mechanism.
How does it increase closure, containment, or enoughness?

6. Why this is different from generic wellness
Explain why this is not just habit tracking, mindfulness, or journaling.

7. Risks or failure modes
How could this feature become annoying, rigid, fake, or triggering?

QUALITY BAR
The ideas must feel:
- emotionally intelligent
- productizable
- specific enough to prototype
- designed for vulnerable late-night states
- less like advice and more like actual UX / feature design

The ideas should be varied across:
- guided UI flows
- sensory transitions
- structured endings
- ritualized closure
- soft prompts
- environmental or pacing support
- aesthetic containment
- post-meal/nighttime landing

Do not stay abstract.
Do not give generic app categories.
Do not repeat the same idea with different names.

At the end, add:
- Top 3 most prototype-worthy ideas
- Why those 3 are strongest for this user
