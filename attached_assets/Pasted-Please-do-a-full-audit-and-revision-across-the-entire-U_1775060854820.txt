Please do a full audit and revision across the entire Untangle app for this recurring structural problem:

The layers are still not separated enough logically.

Right now, many flows appear to have two layers, but layer 2 often acts like a reinterpretation or correction of layer 1, instead of a true expansion.
This makes the experience feel like:
- the user gives a signal
- the app tells them what the signal “really means”

That creates the same class of problem across multiple branches:
- narrowing too early
- overcommitting to one interpretation
- sounding too definitive
- collapsing complexity too quickly
- making the second layer feel like a verdict instead of a gentle opening

Core principle to apply everywhere:
Layer 1 = the user’s live signal / entry point / what is active right now
Layer 2 = a gentle expansion of what may be making that signal feel heavy

Very important:
Layer 2 should NOT sound like:
- “You’re not really X, you’re actually Y”
- “This is really about...”
- “What’s actually happening is...”
- a smarter reinterpretation of the user’s first signal
- a replacement of the user’s wording with the model’s “truer” explanation

Layer 2 SHOULD sound more like:
- “Part of what may make this hard is...”
- “This can feel heavier when...”
- “Sometimes this kind of moment carries...”
- “It may not be only about X — it may also carry...”
- “Part of the knot may be...”
- “It may feel loaded because...”

Goal:
Make layer 1 feel valid on its own.
Make layer 2 feel like added depth, not correction or replacement.

- Quick untangle outputs
- Follow-up questions
- Untangle moments
- Stop lines
- CTA transitions
- Typed input interpretation
- Any “go deeper / different angle” logic
- Any branch where the app currently over-explains or over-defines the user

Please specifically review for these repeated issues:
1. first tap being treated as a conclusion instead of a signal

3. “not X, actually Y” sentence structure

5. insight that sounds smart but does not create landing

8. branches that preserve less emotional openness than others

Please revise the whole app with these design principles:
- First tap = signal, not conclusion
- Each branch = lens, not verdict
- Layer 1 = what is active
- Layer 2 = what may be adding weight
- Insight should lead toward softening, not overclassification
- Naming should create room, not collapse complexity
- Untangle should feel like it is helping the user loosen a knot, not out-explain them

Also include this branch in the audit and keep it as a supported before-eating theme:
deserving / permission / qualification around eating

Core pattern:
Sometimes before eating, the user is not just deciding whether to eat.
She is caught in a deeper loop about whether she is allowed to want food, allowed to satisfy desire, or allowed to respond to appetite without qualification.

This can sound like:
- not hungry, no qualification to eat
- I want it, but I don’t feel allowed to
- I don’t feel justified enough to eat this
- wanting it already feels wrong
- I feel like I need a reason to eat
- I’m treating hunger like it needs proof

Important:
This is not just indecision.
This is not just appetite confusion.
This is a deserving / permission / qualification loop.

the live signal itself is already meaningful and should stand on its own

should gently expand the weight inside it, without replacing it with a more “correct” explanation
