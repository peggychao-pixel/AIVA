Please fix this important safety / interpretation issue across Untangle.

Current problem:
Some lines such as “It’s okay to feel unsettled” are too ambiguous and can accidentally normalize or soothe away a state that may actually reflect unmet physical need.

This is risky because “unsettled” can mean very different things:
1. emotional aftershock / rumination / guilt / mental looping
2. still hungry / not physically satisfied / meal did not land / body not actually held

These are not the same.
If the user is physically unsatisfied, not full, or still not landed in a body-level way, a soft acceptance line like “It’s okay to feel unsettled” can be misread as:
- it’s okay that you didn’t eat enough
- it’s okay to stay underfed
- it’s okay to stop here even though the body is still unmet

That is not acceptable.

Please add a stronger distinction in interpretation logic:
Do not treat all “unsettled” states as emotions to soothe.
Sometimes unsettledness is information that something still has not landed.

New rule:
If the user may still be physically unmet, still hungry, not full, or not actually satisfied,
the response must not jump too quickly to acceptance / closure / stop-line language.

Instead:
- acknowledge that the unsettled feeling may reflect unmet need
- avoid spiritualizing or normalizing underfed states
- avoid making “still not okay” sound like something to simply tolerate
- differentiate emotional looping from bodily incompletion

Helpful language direction:
- “If this still feels unsettled because you’re not actually satisfied, that matters.”
- “Not everything that feels unresolved is a thought loop. Sometimes something still hasn’t landed.”
- “If your body still feels unheld, this is not just something to breathe past.”
- “Some unsettledness is emotional aftermath. Some unsettledness means the need is still there.”

Please review all branches for similar ambiguity, especially:
- after eating flows
- fullness / satisfaction branches
- stop lines
- closure CTAs
- any branch that currently moves too fast from ‘not landed’ to ‘acceptance’

Goal:
Untangle should not accidentally encourage restriction, under-eating, or dismissing unmet need by wrapping it in soothing language.
