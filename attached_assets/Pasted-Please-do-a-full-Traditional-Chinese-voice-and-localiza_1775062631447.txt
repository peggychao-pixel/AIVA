Main problem:
The current Chinese version often feels too literal, too flat, too translated-from-English, and not psychologically alive enough.
Even when the meaning is technically understandable, the tone can feel weak, watery, unnatural, or unlike something a real user would feel or say internally in Chinese.

This is NOT just a translation accuracy issue.
It is a voice, tone, and emotional-language quality issue.

Please audit and revise all Traditional Chinese copy across the app, including:
- option labels
- follow-up questions
- Untangle moments
- stop lines / anchor lines
- CTA buttons
- quick untangle outputs
- typed-input interpretations
- before eating / after eating branches
- deeper-angle flows
- saved moment language
- any bilingual or mirrored EN/TC branch logic

What to look for:
1. overly literal translation from English
2. phrasing that is grammatically correct but emotionally weak
3. wording that does not sound like real inner language in Traditional Chinese
4. abstract phrasing that sounds translated rather than felt
5. unnatural collocations, especially around emotion, shame, desire, guilt, regret, heaviness, qualification, and inner conflict
6. sentences that “mean the right thing” but do not land naturally in Chinese
7. places where the Chinese feels thinner, flatter, or less precise than the English
8. lines that lose the emotional mechanism and become vague

Important product principle:
The Traditional Chinese version should not sound like a direct translation of an English therapeutic interface.
It should sound like emotionally precise, natural, low-shame inner-language for a Traditional Chinese-speaking user.

Tone goals for Traditional Chinese:
- natural
- emotionally exact
- grounded
- low-shame
- not preachy
- not clinical
- not overly literary
- not self-help cheesy
- not therapist-jargon
- not AI-translated sounding

Please preserve Untangle’s product voice:
- psychologically sharp
- gentle but not soft in a vague way
- clear but not robotic
- emotionally meaningful
- concise enough to scan on mobile

Specific instruction:
Do not mirror English sentence structure when it creates weak Chinese.
Rewrite for native-feeling Chinese, even if the structure changes.

Examples of the problem:
Current Chinese can sound like:
- 「你不只是感到罪惡」
- 「心裡有一個重的標準在檢驗你」
- 「不舒服，不等於做錯。」

These may be understandable but still feel translated, thin, or awkward.

Preferred direction:
Use Chinese that feels like actual inner-language, for example:
- 「你卡住的不只是罪惡感」
- 「比較像是心裡有一套很嚴的標準在審你」
- 「難受，不代表你真的做錯了」

Please apply this kind of revision logic everywhere, not just in this one branch.
