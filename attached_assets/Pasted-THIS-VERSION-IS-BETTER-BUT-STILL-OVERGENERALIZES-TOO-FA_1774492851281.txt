THIS VERSION IS BETTER, BUT STILL OVERGENERALIZES TOO FAST

This version is a meaningful improvement:
- it no longer repeats the loop as the stop line
- it goes one layer deeper
- it is less sarcastic
- it is less obviously broken

However, two problems remain:

1. It escalates too quickly into generic identity-level psychology.
2. Language consistency is still not fully fixed.

==================================================
1. DO NOT JUMP TO SELF-WORTH TOO FAST
==================================================

In regret flows, do not automatically escalate to:
- worth
- not being enough
- identity-level inadequacy
- “this reflects badly on me”

unless the user has actually indicated that.

Fear of regret does NOT always mean:
- low self-worth
- fear of being a failure
- not enoughness

Sometimes it means:
- fear of replay
- fear of choosing a version that won’t count
- fear of missing a better option
- fear of wasting the opportunity
- fear of being stuck with the choice

These are different mechanisms.

New rule:
before escalating to identity/self-worth layer, check whether the user has actually signaled:
- shame
- worthlessness
- “what this says about me”
- fear of being fundamentally inadequate

If not, keep the deeper layer closer to the decision mechanism.

==================================================
2. PREFER MECHANISM-LEVEL DEPTH BEFORE IDENTITY-LEVEL DEPTH
==================================================

For “I’m worried I’ll regret it,” better deeper directions are:

- You may not be trying to avoid regret itself.
  You may be trying to avoid getting stuck replaying the choice afterward.

- The harder part may not be making the wrong choice.
  It may be getting trapped with a version you won’t let yourself move on from.

- This may not be about failure in general.
  It may be that once you choose, you keep reopening the decision instead of letting it close.

These are more specific and less generic than:
- this reflects badly on you
- it means you are not enough
- regret doesn’t define your worth

==================================================
3. STOP LINE SHOULD MATCH THE ACTUAL DEPTH LEVEL
==================================================

If the deeper layer is still mechanism-level,
the stop line should stay mechanism-level too.

Do NOT jump from:
decision anxiety
to
identity-worth stop line

That feels mismatched.

For this case, better stop lines would be:
- I don’t need a regret-proof option to choose.
- The job is to choose, not to solve all future regret first.
- I can choose without reopening the decision over and over.
- 我現在不用先找到不會後悔的版本，才能選。
- 現在要做的是選，不是先把後悔清乾淨。
- 選完之後，我不一定要一直重開這個決定。
